UCL

The UEFA Champions League (UCL) is the most prestigious club competition in European football.

Made in 1955 as the European Champion Clubs' Cup, it was rebranded as the UEFA Champions League in 1992. The tournament features top-division European clubs competing for the coveted title. Madrid holds the record for the most UCL titles, with 15 victories as of 2026.

The competition begins with a league stage, which is a group of 36 teams which compete against each other to make the top 8 teams to automatically qualify for the UCL knockouts. The teams from 9th place to 24th go through a playoff round, and the teams from 25th place to 36th are eliminated from the UCL. It is then followed by knockout rounds. The first knockout round is the Round of 16 teams, followed by the quarterfinals, then the semifinals, and finally the final.

Champions League Winners

The UEFA Champions League has seen a variety of winners over the years. Some of the most successful clubs in the competition's history include Real Madrid, AC Milan, Liverpool, Bayern Munich, and FC Barcelona. These clubs have not only won multiple titles but have also produced memorable moments and legendary players who have left a lasting impact on the tournament.

UCL Anthem

The UEFA Champions League anthem, officially titled "Champions League," is a piece of music composed by Tony Britten in 1992. The anthem is based on George Frideric Handel's "Zadok the Priest," which is one of the four crowning anthems composed for the crowning of King George II in 1727.
The UCL anthem is played before the start of each Champions League match, during the opening ceremony, and at various other moments in the UCL.
