UCL

The UEFA Champions League (UCL) is the most prestigious club competition in European football.

Established in 1955 as the European Champion Clubs' Cup, it was rebranded as the UEFA Champions League in 1992. The tournament features top-division European clubs competing for the coveted title. Real Madrid holds the record for the most UCL titles, with 15 victories as of 2026.

The competition begins with a league stage, which is a group of 36 teams which compete against each other to make the top 8 teams to automatically qualify for the UCL knockouts. The teams from 9th place to 24th go through a playoff round, and the teams from 25th place to 36th are eliminated from the UCL. It is then followed by knockout rounds. The first knockout round is the Round of 16 teams, followed by the quarterfinals, then the semifinals, and finally the final.
